About VelociRX

VelociRCX is designed to help consumers learn more about the the side effects and potentially harmful interactions that can occur when taking prescription and over the counter medications. The United Stated Food and Drug Administration tracks adverse reactions and recalls that are reported for drugs, both at the generic and brand specific level. This data is available to the public, and VelociRX helps users examine that data in an easy-to-use way.

Food and Drug Administration

VelociRX leverages the openFDA project, which makes the FDA's database of adverse drug reactions and drug recalls open and available to the public. When a VelociRX user performs a search, that search is done directly against the FDA's latest registry, so the data is always up to date.

For more information about the FDA's publicly available data, visit open.fda.gov

National Institute of Health

VelociRX leverages National Library of Medicine's drug interactions API to retrieve known drug interactions between two types of drugs. This data is made available to the public via the RXNav API.

To learn more about NIH's RXNav service, visit rxnav.nlm.nih.gov/APIsOverview.html
